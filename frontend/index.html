FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS

The six pillars
of your freedom.

Articles 12–35 of the Indian Constitution guarantee these rights to every citizen.

14 — 18
⚖️

Right to Equality

No discrimination. Equal protection under law.

The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or equal protection of the laws within the territory of India.

  • Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex or place of birth
  • Equality of opportunity in matters of public employment
  • Abolition of untouchability
  • Abolition of titles
LANDMARK CASE Indra Sawhney v. Union of India, 1992 — Established limits on reservations at 50%
19 — 22
🕊️

Right to Freedom

Speech, expression, movement, and personal liberty.

Every citizen has the right to freedom of speech and expression, assembly, association, movement, residence, and profession.

  • Freedom of speech and expression
  • Right to assemble peacefully without arms
  • Protection against arbitrary arrest and detention
  • Right to life and personal liberty (Article 21)
LANDMARK CASE Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India, 1978 — Expanded Article 21 to include the right to live with dignity
23 — 24
🛡️

Right Against Exploitation

Protection from forced labour and child labour.

The Constitution prohibits all forms of forced labour, human trafficking, and employment of children in hazardous occupations.

  • Prohibition of traffic in human beings and forced labour
  • Prohibition of employment of children below 14 years in factories
  • Protection from bonded labour
  • Right to fair wages
LANDMARK CASE People's Union for Democratic Rights v. Union of India, 1982 — Defined forced labour broadly
25 — 28
🕌

Freedom of Religion

Freedom of conscience and free practice of religion.

All persons are equally entitled to freedom of conscience and the right to freely profess, practise and propagate religion.

  • Right to freely profess, practise and propagate religion
  • Freedom to manage religious affairs
  • No religious instruction in State-funded institutions
  • Freedom from religious taxation
LANDMARK CASE S.R. Bommai v. Union of India, 1994 — Upheld secularism as a basic structure
29 — 30
📚

Cultural & Educational Rights

Protection of language, script, and culture.

Every section of citizens with a distinct language, script or culture shall have the right to conserve the same.

  • Right of minorities to conserve their language and culture
  • Right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions
  • Protection against discrimination in admissions
  • State shall not discriminate in granting aid to minority institutions
LANDMARK CASE T.M.A. Pai Foundation v. State of Karnataka, 2002 — Defined minority educational rights
Article 32
🏛️

Constitutional Remedies

The right to enforce your rights. The heart of the Constitution.

Dr. Ambedkar called Article 32 the heart and soul of the Constitution. It gives you the right to move the Supreme Court directly if your rights are violated.

  • Habeas Corpus — produce the body, challenge illegal detention
  • Mandamus — court orders authority to perform its duty
  • Prohibition — prevents lower courts from exceeding jurisdiction
  • Certiorari — higher court reviews lower court decisions
LANDMARK CASE Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala, 1973 — Basic structure doctrine protecting fundamental rights
